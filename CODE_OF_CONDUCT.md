## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

![Community Values](https://frost-leo.github.io/Leviftas/assets/images/github/code-of-conduct/values-title.svg)

### Respect
- Treat all community members with dignity and courtesy
- Value different perspectives and experiences
- Be patient with newcomers and those learning
- Acknowledge and appreciate contributions from all skill levels

### Inclusivity
- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Respect pronouns and chosen names
- Make space for underrepresented voices
- Consider the global and diverse nature of our community

### Collaboration
- Focus on constructive feedback and solutions
- Share knowledge and help others learn
- Work together towards common goals
- Credit others for their contributions and ideas

### Professionalism
- Keep discussions relevant and on-topic
- Separate technical disagreements from personal conflicts
- Maintain confidentiality when appropriate
- Represent the project positively in public forums## Expec
ted Behavior

> [!TIP]
> These behaviors help create a positive environment for everyone:

- **Be respectful** in all communications and interactions
- **Be inclusive** and welcoming to newcomers
- **Be collaborative** and constructive in discussions
- **Be professional** in conduct and communication
- **Be patient** when helping others or asking for help
- **Be open** to feedback and different perspectives
- **Give credit** where credit is due
- **Focus on solutions** rather than blame

## Unacceptable Behavior

> [!WARNING]
> The following behaviors are not tolerated in our community:

### Harassment and Discrimination
- Offensive comments related to personal characteristics
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Discriminatory jokes, language, or imagery

### Disruptive Behavior
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Sustained disruption of discussions or events

### Inappropriate Content
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Sharing inappropriate sexual or violent content
- Spamming or excessive self-promotion
- Off-topic discussions that derail conversations

### Technical Misconduct
- Deliberately introducing malicious code
- Violating intellectual property rights
- Sharing credentials or security vulnerabilities inappropriately
- Misrepresenting technical facts or capabilities#
# Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories and discussions
- Issue trackers and pull requests
- Community forums and chat channels
- Social media interactions related to the project
- Conference presentations and meetups
- Any other forums where community members represent the project

![Reporting Guidelines](https://frost-leo.github.io/Leviftas/assets/images/github/code-of-conduct/reporting-title.svg)

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it promptly:

> [!NOTE]
> **Primary Contact**: conduct@leviftas.org
> 
> **Direct Contact**: frostleo.dev@gmail.com (for sensitive matters)

### What to Include

When reporting an incident, please provide:

- **What happened**: A clear description of the incident
- **When it occurred**: Date and time (with timezone if possible)
- **Where it happened**: Platform, channel, or location
- **Who was involved**: Usernames or identities of people involved
- **Evidence**: Screenshots, links, or other supporting materials
- **Impact**: How the incident affected you or others

### Our Response Process

> [!TIP]
> We follow a structured process to ensure fair and thorough handling:

1. **Acknowledgment** (24-48 hours)
   - Confirm receipt of your report
   - Provide a case reference number
   - Outline next steps

2. **Investigation** (3-7 business days)
   - Review all provided information
   - Interview involved parties if necessary
   - Gather additional context as needed
   - Maintain confidentiality throughout

3. **Resolution** (varies by complexity)
   - Determine appropriate response
   - Implement corrective actions
   - Communicate outcome to reporter
   - Follow up to ensure resolution

4. **Follow-up** (ongoing)
   - Monitor for retaliation
   - Check in with affected parties
   - Review and improve processes## E
nforcement

### Possible Consequences

Depending on the severity and nature of the violation, consequences may include:

- **Warning**: Private written warning with clarification of expectations
- **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary ban from community spaces
- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from all community spaces
- **Legal Action**: Involvement of law enforcement for serious violations

### Appeals Process

If you believe an enforcement action was taken in error:

1. Email conduct@leviftas.org within 30 days
2. Include your case reference number
3. Explain why you believe the action was incorrect
4. Provide any additional relevant information

Appeals will be reviewed by uninvolved maintainers within 14 business days.

## Community Guidelines

### For Contributors

- Read and follow our [Contributing Guidelines](CONTRIBUTING.md)
- Use clear, descriptive commit messages and PR titles
- Test your changes thoroughly before submitting
- Be responsive to feedback and review comments
- Help maintain code quality and documentation

### For Maintainers

- Lead by example in following this Code of Conduct
- Provide timely and constructive feedback
- Be transparent about decision-making processes
- Support and mentor new contributors
- Maintain project standards while being welcoming

### For Community Members

- Help newcomers get started
- Share knowledge and resources
- Participate constructively in discussions
- Report issues and suggest improvements
- Respect maintainer decisions and project direction#
